Hi!

I’m Timmy (or Tim), but I go by ralismark on the internet. I’m a 1st year computer science/maths student at UNSW.

On my blog, I (irregularly) post about competitive programming which sometimes goes into the more theoretical parts of computer science, other computing things and the occasional mathy post.

Things I’ve done

  • I’m a lab assistant for COMP1521, a course teaching the low-level fundamentals of how computers work, including assembly (MIPS) and the POSIX APIs.

  • I was a live-in tutor for the 2019 December AIOC School of Excellence, where I taught problem solving skills and advanced applications of algorithms & data structures. I also helped organise and run practice exams and other camp activities.

  • I’ve contributed to Tectonic Typesetting, a LaTeX engine written in Rust & C, submitting PRs to upgrade diagnostic reporting, add an unstable flag system, and integrate cbindgen.
  • I created a game using HTML Canvas & JavaScript – You can play it here
  • I represented Australia in the 2019 Interational Olympiad of Informatics, winning a silver medal.
